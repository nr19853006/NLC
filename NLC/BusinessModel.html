Business Models

Fixed Cost Model / Project-Based Outsourcing
  • The scope of the project is precisely defined
  • Changes in SOW are treated with separate estimation and prior budget approval
  • The project is devoid of external dependencies & follows straightforward timelines
  • The customer is billed on pre-defined milestones
  • Involves the technology that is fairly relevant in the market
  • Ideal for one-off projects that are required by the end customers
Dedicated Resource / Client-Managed Projects
  • The scope of the project is non-decided and efforts are difficult to measure
  • Involves emerging technology and long term engagement
  • Projects with open-ended SOW that require iterative development
  • Flexibility to change team size based on project requirements
  • Involves parallel development of hardware and software or development teams
  • Suits for Proof of Concept based projects & billing is at fixed price
Managed Services/ Team Model
  • Similar to dedicated resource but we will manage the project along with client
  • Resource has greater access to team shared resourcecs and available expertise.
Time & Material
  • Project scope is partially clear and efforts cannot be estimated
  • Involves evolving concepts and frequently changing parameters
  • Nature of work is intermittent in nature
  • Projects that require incremental development
  • Suitable for support and maintenance of small to mid-sized applications
  • Billing is charged based on the actual amount of time & efforts spent
Build-Operate-Transfer (BOT)
  • In this model, the solutions provider operates the business over a period of time and then later on transfers the operations to the client.
Customized Solution
  • Client tells us what he want and we will evaluate for feasibility and costing
Consultancy Services
  • We act as consultant between client and his development partner or help them integrating effort between all the vendors.

How to select best model for your Business

Your specific needs will dictate the type of outsourcing model that works best for your business.

If the operation is permanent — a large round-the-clock customer service team, for example — a Build-Operate-Transfer model will prove the best long-term investment.

If the need for skilled staff is a temporary one or a flexible on-going relationship is required (SEO, content marketing, and digital marketing services often require this), a combination of offshoring and staff augmentation is the best solution. This provides access to industry experts at a lower rate. What’s more, services are flexible: payments can be arranged on a fixed price basis or on a pay-as-you-go model.
